tr-agent

v0.6

A local AI paper-trading agent that runs entirely on your machine — no cloud, no subscriptions.

How it works

Every trading day, the agent scans 48 large-cap stocks, selects the best setups each morning, then paper-trades them every 30 minutes through a 4-stage pipeline: technical signals → risk check → LLM confirmation → order execution. A LightGBM model runs in the background, learning from every closed trade and improving its confidence scores over time.

Stop-Loss Enforcement

Runs before everything else on every open position.

Fetch current price for the held position.

1

Signal Detection (pure Python)

Fetches 1 year of daily OHLCV from yfinance, computes indicators, derives a signal.

RSI(14), MACD(12/26/9), SMA(20/50/200), ATR, Bollinger Bands, ADX, ROC, volume ratio.

Queries LightGBM → ml_confidence (0–1 probability of profitable outcome).

BUY when ≥2 of: RSI < 30 · MACD histogram > 0 · SMA20 > SMA50.

SELL when ≥2 of: RSI > 70 · MACD histogram < 0 · SMA20 < SMA50. Otherwise NEUTRAL.

Stage 1b — BUY Guards (applied before risk check)

Market regime: if SPY SMA50 < SMA200 (death cross) → suppress BUY.

Earnings blackout: if earnings within 3 days → suppress BUY. Fails closed on API error.

2

Risk Check (pure Python)

Max 20% of cash per single trade.

Max 60% of portfolio invested at any one time.

SELL requires an open position in that ticker.

Rejections trigger a Telegram alert; ticker is skipped for this cycle.

3

LLM Confirmation (Ollama · qwen2.5:7b)

The LLM receives full context and decides whether to confirm, modify, or reject the signal.

Input: signal type · all indicators (incl. SMA200) · ML confidence · market regime (golden/death cross) · portfolio state · recent trade history.

Output: JSON {confirmed, quantity, reasoning}.

Conservative by design — rejects anything ambiguous. ML confidence shown as "supports/warns against trade."

🟢 ML > 60%: "supports trade"    🟡 40–60%: neutral    🔴 < 40%: "warns against trade"
4

Order Execution (PaperBroker)

Fill at current market price ± 0.1% slippage simulation.

Persist to data/portfolio_state.json.

Log every event to data/journal.db (SQLite).

Send Telegram notification with P&L summary.

Training Loop

Bootstrap: 2 years of history across 5+ tickers (~480 labeled samples).

Glossary — Key Concepts

Ticker

A short code that uniquely identifies a publicly traded company on the stock market.

AAPL Apple
NVDA Nvidia
GOOGL Google
META Meta

Every company in the 48-ticker candidate pool is referenced by its ticker throughout the codebase, journal, and Telegram alerts.

SPY

A special ticker that tracks the S&P 500 — a basket of the 500 largest US companies. It moves with the overall US stock market.

Used in two ways in this agent:

Regime filter: if SPY SMA50 < SMA200 (death cross), the whole market is in a downtrend — BUY signals are suppressed.

ML features: rel_roc_5 measures if a stock is outperforming the market; spy_corr_60 measures how closely it moves with SPY.

OHLCV — What a Day of Stock Data Looks Like

For every ticker, yfinance returns 365 rows of this — one per trading day over the past year. All indicators are computed from these numbers.

Indicators — Reading the Market's Signals

Mathematical formulas applied to OHLCV data to summarize what's happening with a stock's price. They don't predict the future — they describe the current state so the agent can make a rule-based decision.

IndicatorWhat it measuresHow we use it
RSI
Relative Strength Index		 Is the stock overbought or oversold? Scale 0–100. BUY signal when RSI < 30 (oversold). SELL when RSI > 70 (overbought).
MACD
Moving Avg Convergence Divergence		 Is momentum building upward or downward? MACD histogram > 0 = upward momentum → contributes to BUY signal.
SMA 20/50/200
Simple Moving Average		 Average closing price over the last N days. Smooths out noise. SMA20 > SMA50 = short-term uptrend → BUY. SMA200 shows the long-term trend.
ATR
Average True Range		 How much does the price typically move per day? Measures volatility. ML feature: atr_pct = ATR / price. High ATR = volatile stock.
Bollinger Bands A band around the average price. Is the stock near the top or bottom of its normal range? ML features: bb_position (where in the band) and bb_width (how wide = how volatile).
ADX
Average Directional Index		 Is the stock trending strongly or drifting sideways? Scale 0–100. Screener adds +1.0 score when ADX > 25 (strong trend). Low ADX = choppy, unreliable signals.
ROC
Rate of Change		 How much has the price changed over the last 5/10/20 days? In percent. ML features. Combined with SPY ROC to compute rel_roc_5 (outperformance vs market).
Volume ratio Is today's trading volume above or below the 20-day average? Screener adds +1.0 when ratio > 1.2 — unusual volume often precedes a move.
LightGBM — Where It Lives & What It Does

What is it?

LightGBM is a gradient boosting algorithm — it builds an ensemble of hundreds of small decision trees, each one learning from the mistakes of the previous ones. The result is a model that can find non-linear patterns across all 16 features simultaneously.

It outputs a single number: the probability (0–1) that a trade right now will be profitable. We call this ml_confidence.

Example: RSI = 28 alone is a BUY signal. But if ADX is low (sideways market) and volume is weak and SPY is dropping, LightGBM might output 0.32 — "this setup historically loses money." The LLM sees that warning and may reject the trade.

Where it runs

data/models/
signal_model.pkl The trained model saved to disk. Loaded once at startup, used every cycle.
signals/technical.py
_enrich_with_ml() Called at the end of Stage 1. Takes the 16 computed features, feeds them to the model, attaches ml_confidence to the signal.
⚡ Runs in <1ms per ticker — fast enough to score all 48 tickers during the pre-market screener without noticeable delay.

The Full Journey of ml_confidence

StepWhereHow ml_confidence is used
1. Computed signals/technical.py 16 features from today's OHLCV → LightGBM → probability 0.0–1.0 attached to the signal.
2. Screener scoring screener.py +1.0 score if > 55%, −0.5 if < 40%. Helps rank which of the 48 tickers to put in today's watchlist.
3. LLM prompt agent/prompts.py Shown as "ML model supports trade" / "ML model warns against trade" so the LLM can weigh it. LLM makes the final call — ML is advisory, not a veto.
4. Journal journal.db Logged alongside every signal so the weekly analysis can measure whether high-confidence calls actually won more often.
5. Retraining ml/trainer.py As paper trades close with real P&L, new labeled rows are added to the training set. The model retrains on its own mistakes automatically.
Data Freshness — What Updates When

Not everything updates at the same frequency. Understanding this is key to knowing when the agent's decisions are reliable.

DataUpdates every cycle?Why it matters
RSI, MACD, SMA20, SMA50

SMA200

Stop-Loss — The Expert View

Do professionals use stop-losses?

Yes — but not fixed % stops. A 5% stop on NVDA (which routinely moves 3–4% in a normal day) barely clears the noise. You get stopped out right before the recovery.

What experts avoid is the fixed number, not the concept. The professional approach is to make the threshold adapt to each stock's volatility.

Three professional approaches

ATR-based (what we use): stop = entry − (2 × ATR). NVDA gets ~8% room, JNJ gets ~1.6%. Automatically calibrated per stock.

Trailing stop: moves up as price rises, never down. Locks in profits while still exiting a reversal. Future improvement.

Position sizing: size positions so small that the worst-case loss is acceptable without needing a stop at all. Used by some quant funds.

ATR-based stop in practice

StockTypical daily ATRFixed 5% stopATR-based (2×) stopVerdict
NVDA ~3.8% 5% — barely outside noise ~7.6% below entry ATR gives real breathing room
TSLA ~4.5% 5% — will trigger on normal days ~9% below entry ATR prevents constant stop-hunting
JNJ ~0.7% 5% — too loose, slow to protect ~1.4% below entry ATR exits quickly on a real move
AAPL ~1.5% 5% — somewhat reasonable ~3% below entry ATR still tighter and more accurate

The ATR-based stop_price is computed when a BUY fills and stored on the position. Stage 0 checks it each cycle. Positions opened before this change fall back to the old fixed 5% rule automatically.

LLM Feedback Rules — How the Agent Learns

The problem

The LLM (qwen2.5:7b) is a static model — it can't be fine-tuned locally. Without feedback, it makes each decision from scratch and repeats the same types of mistakes week after week.

Example: if TSLA BUY signals have a 28% win rate over 30 days, the LLM doesn't know that. It evaluates each TSLA signal as if it were the first one.

The solution

Every Sunday, the weekly analysis job now writes data/llm_rules.md — a structured rules file synthesized by Ollama from the last 30 days of trade outcomes. Every future Stage 3 confirmation reads this file and applies the rules.

The LLM now knows: "TSLA BUY signals historically lose — require higher confidence."

You can also edit the file manually between Sundays — your edits survive until the next regeneration.

Rules file format (data/llm_rules.md)

## Learned Rules (generated 2026-05-18 · 47 trades · 30-day window)

### What's working:
- RSI < 30 entries: 68% win rate vs 41% for RSI 30-35 — prefer tight RSI thresholds
- NVDA: 71% win rate across 7 trades — trust signals more than average

### What to avoid:
- TSLA: 28% win rate (2/7) — require higher ML confidence before confirming BUY
- Stop-loss triggered 3× this period (avg -4.2%) — reduce size in volatile conditions

### Suggested constraints:
- Require ML confidence > 55% when ticker win rate < 40%
- Be more conservative when stop-loss exit rate > 40% in recent period
News Injection — Qualitative Context for the LLM

What and why

Technical indicators are purely numeric — they can't detect that NVDA just had an earnings miss, or that a regulatory investigation was announced this morning. News headlines fill that gap.

Before each Stage 3 LLM confirmation, the agent fetches the 2–3 most recent headlines for that ticker (last 48h) via yfinance and injects them into the prompt.

How it works

Source: yf.Ticker(ticker).news — free, no API key, already a dependency.

Filter: only articles published in the last 48 hours.

Cache: one fetch per ticker per day — screener runs 48 tickers without redundant requests.

Limit: max 3 headlines to protect the model's context window.

📰 If no recent news exists → section omitted from prompt. Failures are silent — never blocks a trade.
